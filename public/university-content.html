Beautiful Mathematical Formulas
Euler's Identity
$$e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$$
Called "the most beautiful theorem in mathematics," connecting five fundamental mathematical constants.
Cauchy-Riemann Equations
$$\frac{\partial u}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial v}{\partial y}, \quad \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} = -\frac{\partial v}{\partial x}$$
Fundamental conditions for complex differentiability in complex analysis.
Stokes' Theorem
$$\iint_S (\nabla \times \vec{F}) \cdot d\vec{S} = \oint_C \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r}$$
Relates surface integrals of curl to line integrals around the boundary.
Fundamental Theorem of Algebra
$$p(z) = a_n z^n + a_{n-1} z^{n-1} + \cdots + a_1 z + a_0$$
Every non-constant polynomial has at least one complex root.
Riemann Zeta Function
$$\zeta(s) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^s} = \prod_p \frac{1}{1-p^{-s}}$$
Central to number theory and the distribution of prime numbers.
Maxwell's Equations
$$\nabla \cdot \vec{E} = \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0}, \quad \nabla \times \vec{E} = -\frac{\partial \vec{B}}{\partial t}$$
Fundamental equations of electromagnetism and field theory.
